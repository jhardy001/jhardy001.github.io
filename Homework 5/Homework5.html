Video Gaming for Newbies


Introduction

My name is Jay Hardy, and I am a student at Converse University. Go Valkryies! I want to interest you in video games, much like I was when I was eight years old. I am passionate about video games, and play them anytime I get the chance. It is what inspires me to be a computer scientist, and an artist! I hope to get you, the reader, a convincing article on what video games are, how to get involved, the different types of games there are, the common lingo in the gaming community and my recommendations of the kinds of games you can start with.

What is a video game?

Webster's Dictionary states video games can be defined as "an electronic game in which players control images on a video screen".

They're supposed to be fun for everyone! Personally, my favorite titles are from Nintendo and Sega.

How do I get interested?

  1. Pick up a console and start playing a game of your choice! (I'll go over different genres later on.)
  2. Watch "let's plays" or gameplays on YouTube or watch them live on Twitch.
  3. Interact with other gamers via. online or in-person in classes, conventions, forums, etc.
  4. Read reviews online! Plenty of sites, like IGN or Metacritic offer a plethora of articles on video games.
  5. Wanna get competitive? Join your school's esports team!

Lingo for Gamers

Here are some lingo you'll need to know (mainly for forums and Shooter/MMORPG games).

"Lol"
Lol means "laugh out loud".
"GG"
GG means "good game".
"FTW"
FTW means "for the win".
"AFK"
AFK means "away from keyboard".
